Citizenship Amendment Act

A recent act that has been passed by the Central Government of India is the Citizenship Amendment Act. For the last few weeks, many university students and illiterate people with good or bad intentions in mind have taken part in processions which have gradually led to riots in the country. I have observed that there are misconceptions in students regarding this.

In 1947, our nation was split into two based on religion. Though India chose to be a secular nation respecting all religions, Pakistan chose to be a Muslim nation. Millions of people walked from India to Pakistan and vice versa. However, due to affinity, family and assets, Many Hindus and Sikhs remained in Pakistan and Muslims stayed back in India. The then Prime Ministers of India and Pakistan, Jawaharlal Nehru and Liaquat Ali Khan have assured each other that if the religious minorities want to migrate to the other country, they should be taken care of.

Further, in 1971 Pakistan too split into two nations-- Pakistan and Bangladesh. Bangladesh too chose to be an Islamic country. Though the Muslim minorities in India have been taken care of, the religious minorities in Pakistan and Bangladesh have not been given the same treatment. Due to this, thousands of minorities such as Sikh, Christians, Buddhists and Hindus came into India. But they haven't been given citizenship here also so they became refugees.

The Citizenship Amendment Act is an act that provides citizenship to such people who are persecuted based on religion. However, there has been a cutoff point till 2014 which means that only people who have come before that will be granted citizenship. This precaution has been taken to avoid the flooding of refugees from the countries of Pakistan, Bangladesh and Afghanistan.
